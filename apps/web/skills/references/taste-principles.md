# Taste Lab Principles

Taste Lab designs should feel specific, premium, editable, and attention-aware. The design system exists to help the viewer understand the product quickly, not to display decoration.

## Baseline

- Clear first fixation target.
- Short, strong headline.
- Visible primary CTA.
- Concrete product or event details.
- Structural imagery or object/media treatment.
- Controlled palette with one or two accents.
- Readable foreground colors on the actual background or image crop behind each text node.
- Generous section spacing and readable text.
- Reserved text bounds with no subtle overlaps.
- Persisted nodes that remain editable after reload.

## Anti-Slop Checklist

Avoid:

- Generic SaaS dashboard panels when the product is not a dashboard.
- Purple/blue glow as the default high-end treatment.
- Repeated card rows with no rhythm change.
- Random decorative blobs, orbs, or abstract shapes.
- Fake brand names and placeholder metrics.
- Overpacked hero sections.
- Tiny unreadable labels.
- Dark required text on dark hero backgrounds.
- Text boxes that overlap other text, CTAs, metrics, or image labels.
- Crops that shrink dominant reference imagery into decorative thumbnails.
- Gradient text as a shortcut for premium.
- Copy like "unleash", "elevate", "revolutionize", "next-gen", or "seamless platform".

## Quality Bar

A strong result should answer:

1. What is this product or place?
2. What should I do next?
3. Why should I trust it?
4. What makes this design distinct?
5. Can the canvas nodes be selected and edited?
